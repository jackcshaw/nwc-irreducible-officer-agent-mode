# Vendrell & Johnston 2026: Scaffolding Critical Thinking With Generative AI

## Citation

Vendrell, M., & Johnston, S.-K. (2026). Scaffolding critical thinking with generative AI: Design principles for integrating large language models in higher education. *Computers and Education: Artificial Intelligence, 10*, 100572. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.caeai.2026.100572

## Why It Matters For The Essay

This is one of the clearest education-design sources behind the essay. It supports the argument that AI use is not simply allowed or banned; it has to be designed so students preserve cognitive ownership, evaluate AI outputs, and move deliberately between AI-free and AI-mediated phases.

## Best Uses In The Essay

- Section III: finished artifacts cannot show the whole learning process.
- Section VI: productive friction should be preserved, not automated away by default.
- Section VIII: assessment should align with intended cognition, not surface fluency.
- Section IX: the pilot's sequence matches the paper's design logic.

## Key Ideas To Carry Forward

- Unstructured LLM use can weaken critical thinking through cognitive offloading, metacognitive disengagement, and epistemic narrowing.
- Good pedagogy positions LLMs as provisional thinking partners, not authorities.
- Students should do some interpretive and planning work before AI enters.
- Evaluation should be embedded throughout the workflow.
- Assessment should reward reasoning, critique, and reflection rather than polished output.
- AI-free and AI-mediated phases should be sequenced deliberately.
- Process traces can inform faculty judgment, but should not replace it.

## Useful Design Principles

- Preserve cognitive friction: require students to formulate hypotheses, analyze problems, or generate arguments before consulting AI.
- Treat LLMs as thinking partners: use them to surface alternatives, counterarguments, and questions, while keeping the student responsible for the reasoning.
- Embed evaluation throughout the workflow: students should explain why they accepted, rejected, or revised AI outputs.
- Activate self-regulation: prompt logs, planning templates, and short reflections make the student's thinking visible.
- Align assessment with thinking: reward quality of reasoning, critique, and reflection rather than surface polish.
- Balance AI-free and AI-mediated phases: independent reasoning should precede AI challenge, critique, and revision.

## Caution

The paper is conceptual and normative, not empirical validation of the specific NWC pilot. Use it as a design frame and vocabulary source, not as proof that a particular assignment design will work.
